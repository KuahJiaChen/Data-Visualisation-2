Global Lifestyle
By 2022, there will 195 countries in the world, with a population of almost 8 billion. Every country has a particular way of life, as well as a different economy, cost of living, and standard of living. Thus, it would be quite exciting to analyse the economic, geographic, national, and regional statistics as well as numerous indicators of the citizens' quality of life by developing an appealing visualisation. By taking a look at the visuals made in this dashboard, we can obtain an overview of the data for all countries.
The dataset used to conduct the analysis consists of the information for each country for the period from 1960 to 2020.
Health Care Quality in each country in 2020

Health Care Quality

The bubble plot depicts the correlation between the global healthcare index and the global healthcare expenditure index. The Healthcare Index and the Healthcare Expenditure Index are strongly positively correlated. Consequently, a higher healthcare expenditure index might result in a higher healthcare index, which subsequently gives their population a higher quality of life.

The bubble plot reveals that among all countries, South Korea, which is located in Asia, has the highest healthcare and healthcare expenditure indexes, while Iraq, which is located in South America, has the lowest indexes for both.

We can also observe that most of the populous nations appear to be from Asia based on the size of the points and the filtering feature.

Household Expenditure & Fertility Rate

Household Expenditure

The household spending per capita in USD for each country in 2020 is displayed on the choropleth map. Household expenditures are the sums that local households spend on things like food, clothing, rent, electricity, transportation, medical costs, entertainment, and other goods and services.

On the other side, the United States has the greatest household expenditure ($37903) among all nations, as can be seen from the map. Switzerland ($28320) and Luxembourg ($28261) came next.

Additionally, by choosing a nation from the choropleth map, the line chart for that nation's fertility rate from 1960 to 2016 will be displayed.

Fertility Rate

The line graph displays the fertility rate for a specific country between 1960 and 2016. The total number of live births to women in a given year, represented as a percentage of the total annual population of women, is known as the fertility rate. The majority of the countries fertility rates declined consistently over time, which is surprising. I believe that recent medical developments have caused birth rates to decline in many countries.

Internet Users for the top 10 countries in Africa in 2020

Number of Internet User

In modern society, the internet has gained popularity. On the internet, we can find knowledge and information that will help our individual, societal, and economic development. Thus, the internet's assistance is necessary to meet our needs and achieve our goals every day.

However, it appears that many African residents do not regularly use the Internet. The bar in bar chart displays the proportion of internet users among the populace in the top 10 countries by population in 2020. The red color bar displays the population, while the pink color bar displays the number of internet users for each country. Ethiopia and Congo (the Democratic Republic of the Congo) have the lowest percentage of internet users among these nations, with 18% each. However, Kenya, which is located in Africa, has a comparatively high rate of Internet users (87%).

Life expectancy Rankings for several countries

Life Expectancy

The Bump Chart displays the top 10 countries with the highest population rankings for life expectancy from 1960 to 2010. The term "life expectancy" describes the typical time frame that a person may expect to live.

It is surprising to discover from the bump chart that the United States has the highest life expectancy overall. Additionally, as time goes on, its life expectancy continues to rise. This indicates that Americans do indeed have a high standard of living. We cannot, however, ignore the fact that China's life expectancy is rising steadily. China had the biggest improvement among these countries, going from eighth to third from where it started.

On the other hand, we can also observe that Russia is now ranked lower. In 1960, it was ranked second, but it finally fell to sixth.

Percentage of total population for each country in 2020

The waterfall chart illustrates the percentage of the world's population for the top 10 countries in 2020. In 2020, there will be about 7.6 billion people around the world. Using the graph, we can observe that China and India account for 37% of the world's population, vastly outnumbering all other countries combined (1440297825 and 1382345085, respectively).

The United States and Indonesia come next, with respective rankings of 331341050 and 274021604. It's also noteworthy to note that the population of the top 3 to 10 countries is only roughly equal to half of China and India combined.

Age Structure for several countries in 2020

Age Structure

For the top 5 countries with the highest population in the world, the Sankey charts display the age distribution. Age structure is divided into three groups: 0 to 14 years, 15 to 64 years, and 65 years and older. It's noteworthy to note that of the 2484433000 people living in the five countries, the majority of them fall into the age range of 15 to 64. 0 to 14 years old and 65 and older are followed by 854051230 and 315856390, respectively.

China, which has the largest population in the world, has 254932700, 1032694000, and 158432800 individuals who belong into the age groups of 0 to 14, 15 to 64, and 65 and older, respectively. India, the world's second-largest nation, has 384291900, 915112400, and 82940710 citizens who belong into the age groups of 0 to 14, 15 to 64, and 65 and older, respectively.

Age Structure for three Continents in 2020
The top three continents with the highest population among the six continents in 2020 are represented by donut charts that indicate the age distribution. The donut charts show that the majority of the population on the three continents is between the ages of 15 and 64. But out of all of them, Asia has the greatest percentage—roughly 68%—in this category. Europe and Africa are close behind, coming in at roughly 66% and 56% , respectively.
Cost of Living Index in 2020

Cost of Living

The cost of living index for the top 15 countries around the world is displayed on the lollipop chart. The cost of living is the sum of money required in a certain location and time frame to meet necessities including housing, food, taxes, and medical care.

We can select to visualize the number of countries that will appear on the chart by adjusting the slider underneath. The graphic shows that Bermuda, Switzerland, and Norway are the three nations with the highest cost of living indices worldwide. Their relative cost of living indices is roughly 148, 126, and 97. It's also intriguing to see that all 15 of the top countries have the cost of living indices above 75.
